## The Research Foundation

Our design is grounded in decades of second language acquisition (SLA) research:

### Comprehensible Input Hypothesis (Krashen)
Language is acquired when learners receive input slightly above their current level (i+1). Our AI calibrates every interaction to each student's precise proficiency level.

### Output Hypothesis (Swain)
Production (speaking, writing) forces learners to notice gaps in their knowledge. Our system creates constant, low-stakes opportunities to produce language.

### Interaction Hypothesis (Long)
Negotiation of meaning in conversation drives acquisition. Our AI conversation partners are designed to create natural opportunities for clarification and repair.

### Noticing Hypothesis (Schmidt)
Learners must consciously notice linguistic features to acquire them. Our system highlights patterns, provides just-in-time explanations, and tracks what has been noticed vs. acquired.

### Sociocultural Theory (Vygotsky)
Learning happens in the Zone of Proximal Development through scaffolded interaction. Our AI provides dynamic scaffolding that adjusts in real-time.

### Spaced Repetition (Ebbinghaus)
Vocabulary and structures need systematic review at optimal intervals. Our system manages this automatically, invisibly woven into activities.

## Core Design Principles

### 1. Production Over Passive Consumption

```
Traditional Approach:
- Watch video âœ“
- Read grammar explanation âœ“
- Complete fill-in-the-blank âœ“
- NEVER ACTUALLY SPEAK THE LANGUAGE âœ—

LinguaFlow Approach:
- Every session includes speaking (even 30 seconds counts)
- Writing happens daily (even one sentence)
- Listening is interactive (respond, don't just hear)
- Reading leads to discussion (with AI or peers)
```

### 2. Communicative Competence Over Grammatical Accuracy

Students should be able to **accomplish communicative goals**, not just produce grammatically perfect sentences. We assess:

| Competence Type | What It Means | How We Develop It |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Linguistic** | Grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation | Systematic instruction + practice |
| **Sociolinguistic** | Appropriate language for context | Role-plays, scenario simulations |
| **Discourse** | Coherent extended speech/writing | Story telling, argumentation tasks |
| **Strategic** | Compensating for gaps | Communication strategy training |

### 3. Affective Filter Management

Anxiety kills language acquisition. Our system:
- Creates psychologically safe speaking environments
- Never judges or embarrasses
- Celebrates effort over accuracy
- Provides private practice before public performance
- Allows students to control their exposure level

### 4. Teacher Amplification, Not Replacement

**Good teachers become excellent. Excellent teachers become extraordinary.**

The AI handles:
- Infinite speaking practice opportunities
- Instant feedback on pronunciation
- Vocabulary drilling and spaced repetition
- Progress tracking and gap identification
- Assessment generation and marking

The teacher focuses on:
- Complex explanations and cultural nuance
- Motivating and inspiring students
- Managing class dynamics and differentiation
- High-stakes assessment validation
- Authentic communication experiences
