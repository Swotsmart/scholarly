Intelligence at Every Layer

AI that serves learners, not surveils them

Traditional EdTech is transactional â€” book a tutor, generate a timetable. Scholarly inverts this: every service incorporates AI that learns, predicts, and adapts. But always with the learner's consent and control.

1

Privacy-First Intelligence

Learners and parents control exactly what data flows between services through explicit Privacy Settings. A parent might allow the Knowledge Graph to inform tutor matching while keeping emotional state data private.

2

Gap-to-Tutor Pipeline

When the LIS detects a knowledge gap, it doesn't just flag it â€” it translates that gap into actionable recommendations: the best tutors for that specific concept, the most effective content, and the optimal learning approach.

3

Affective-Aware Scheduling

The scheduling engine respects emotional state when planning. If a learner is fatigued, it won't schedule their hardest subject next. The system understands that cognition and emotion are inseparable.

4

Predictive Interventions

The system continuously scans for learners who may be struggling â€” detecting patterns across engagement, performance, and behaviour long before traditional measures like failing grades would flag a concern.
